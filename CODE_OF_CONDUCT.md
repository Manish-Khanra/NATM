# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We want NATM to be a welcoming, respectful, and constructive space for people
working on transport transition modeling, agent-based simulation, data
preprocessing, and open research software.

Participants are expected to communicate with care, assume good faith, and help
maintain a collaborative environment.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful and constructive.
- Welcome questions and different experience levels.
- Give technical feedback without personal criticism.
- Credit ideas, code, data, and documentation work appropriately.
- Be mindful that transport, climate, policy, and infrastructure modeling can
  involve many disciplines and perspectives.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, intimidation, or personal attacks.
- Discriminatory language or behavior.
- Deliberate disruption of discussions or project work.
- Publishing private information without permission.
- Misrepresenting authorship, data provenance, or licensing.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may remove comments, close issues, or restrict
participation when behavior is harmful to the project community.

If you need to report a concern, open a private communication channel with the
project maintainer where available.
